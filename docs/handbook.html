Chapter 1 Introduction

DentVault is a cross-platform desktop application for independent dental practitioners and small clinics. All data is stored locally on your device in a secure vault folder you control — no cloud, no subscriptions, no third-party servers.

What DentVault Does

  • Patient management — demographics, medical history, status tracking
  • Clinical timeline — chronological treatment history with structured categories and outcomes
  • Treatment plans — multi-step plans with cost tracking and completion progress
  • Dental chart — visual SVG tooth chart with per-surface tagging, bridges, prosthetics
  • Periodontal probing — full 6-point probing with recession, CAL, BOP, mobility, furcation
  • Documents — file attachments organised by configurable categories
  • Analytics — practice-level outcome statistics, provider performance, trend reports

Clinical Intelligence Philosophy

DentVault is not just a record system — it is a clinical outcome engine. Every treatment entry is tagged with a structured category and outcome so you can ask questions like:

  • "Of all root canals performed in the last 3 years, what percentage were retreated?"
  • "Show all Class II patients — what was the post-treatment occlusal result?"
  • "Which provider has the highest extraction rate after crown placement?"

This is only possible because every piece of clinical data is structured and queryable — never buried in free-text notes.

Data Ownership

Your data, your vault All records are stored in a single folder on your computer called the vault. You can back it up, move it to an external drive, or archive it. DentVault never transmits your patient data anywhere.

Chapter 2 Getting Started

First Launch — Vault Setup

The first time you open DentVault, you will be prompted to choose a vault location. This is the folder where all patient data, documents, and the audit log will be stored.

  1. Click Choose Vault Folder in the welcome screen.
  2. Navigate to a folder on your computer (e.g. Documents/DentVault).
  3. Click Select. DentVault initialises the database inside that folder.
  4. The main interface loads. You are ready to add your first patient.
Backup Recommendation Store your vault on a folder that is synced by iCloud, Dropbox, or a similar service — or schedule regular manual backups. DentVault does not backup automatically.

Chapter 3 Managing Patients

The Patient List

The Patients page is your main directory. It shows all patients with their status, last visit date, and a quick search bar.

Patient Statuses

StatusMeaning
ActiveCurrently receiving treatment or on recall
InactiveNot seen recently; may need recall contact
ArchivedNo longer a patient; record retained for history
DeceasedDeceased patient; record locked

Creating a New Patient

  1. Click the New Patient button (top-right of the Patients page).
  2. Enter the patient's first and last name (required).
  3. Fill in demographics: date of birth, gender, contact details, address.
  4. Add medical history flags, allergies, and any relevant conditions.
  5. Click Save Patient. The patient profile opens immediately.

Patient Profile Overview

Each patient profile is divided into tabs accessible from the toolbar at the top of the patient page:

Tab / SectionContents
TimelineChronological clinical entries, inline images, document links
Treatment PlansMulti-step planned procedures with cost and status
Dental ChartVisual tooth chart — conditions, surfaces, bridges
PerioPeriodontal probing records over time
DocumentsAttached files, X-rays, referral letters, photos
Quick search tip The patient search bar matches on name, ID, and phone number. Press / anywhere in the app to focus the search bar instantly.

Chapter 4 Clinical Timeline

The timeline is the heart of a patient's record — a chronological log of every clinical encounter, observation, and treatment. Each entry is a structured record, not just a free-text note.

Adding a New Entry

  1. Click the + Add Entry button in the timeline toolbar.
  2. Type your clinical note in the text field. As you type, the smart tag suggestion bar appears below if it detects clinical keywords.
  3. Select a treatment category from the dropdown (or accept the suggestion).
  4. Optionally set a treatment outcome and tooth numbers.
  5. Tag the primary doctor and any colleagues involved.
  6. Click Save or press Ctrl+Enter.

Treatment Categories

Every entry must be tagged with one of the following categories. These are used for analytics and outcome tracking.

CategoryTypical Procedures
EndodonticsRoot canal, retreatment, apicoectomy, pulpotomy, pulp cap
OrthodonticsFixed appliances, aligners, retainers, space maintainers
ProsthodonticsCrowns, bridges, dentures, veneers, implant prosthetics
PeriodonticsScaling, root planing, flap surgery, implant placement
Oral SurgeryExtractions, biopsies, frenectomy, impacted third molars
RestorativeComposite, amalgam fillings, inlays, onlays
PreventiveCleaning, sealants, fluoride, check-ups
ImagingPeriapical, panoramic, CBCT, cephalometric

Treatment Outcomes

Recording outcomes is what makes DentVault's analytics meaningful. Set the outcome when treatment is complete or at follow-up:

OutcomeWhen to use
SuccessfulTreatment achieved its goal; tooth/area is stable
OngoingTreatment in progress or too early to evaluate
RetreatedSame procedure had to be repeated on the same tooth
Failed → ExtractedTreatment failed; tooth was subsequently extracted
Failed → OtherTreatment failed; alternative treatment was performed

Smart Tag Suggestions

As you type a clinical note, DentVault scans for keywords and surfaces non-intrusive tag chips below the text field. You can Accept or Dismiss each suggestion — tags are never applied automatically.

If you type…Suggested tag
root canal, RCT, endodonticCategory: Endodontics
crown, bridge, veneerCategory: Prosthodontics
extraction, surgical extractCategory: Oral Surgery
retreatment, re-treatmentOutcome: Retreated + Related Entry chip
extracted due toOutcome: Failed → Extracted + Related Entry chip
tooth 14, #14, FDI 24Tooth number tag
Negation detection Phrases like "no signs of caries" or "ruled out periodontitis" suppress the corresponding suggestion — DentVault will not suggest a Periodontics tag if the note says there is no periodontal involvement.

Doctor & Colleague Tagging

Each entry can be attributed to a primary doctor (selected from a dropdown) and one or more colleagues (using the @ mention palette in the text field).

  1. In the entry text field, type @ followed by the first letters of a staff member's name.
  2. A palette appears — click the name or press Enter to insert the mention.
  3. The mentioned colleague is tagged on the entry and appears in doctor attribution reports.

Text Block Shortcuts

Pre-defined text blocks can be inserted into any entry using the / command palette. For example, typing /postop might expand to your standard post-operative instructions. Manage text blocks in Settings → Text Blocks.

Attachments on Entries

Drag an image file onto an entry or use the attachment button to add X-rays, intraoral photos, or scanned documents directly to a timeline entry. Images display as inline thumbnails that can be expanded with a click.

Chapter 5 Treatment Plans

Treatment plans allow you to outline a proposed sequence of procedures for a patient, track completion, and record estimated costs. A plan is separate from the timeline — it represents planned work, while timeline entries record what has actually been done.

Creating a Treatment Plan

  1. In the patient profile, open the Treatment Plans section.
  2. Click New Plan and give it a descriptive name (e.g. "Full mouth rehabilitation Q2 2026").
  3. Add plan items — each item has a description, tooth number(s), estimated cost, and ordering.
  4. Save the plan. Items are listed in sequence with checkboxes.

Completing Plan Items

When a procedure from the plan is carried out, mark the plan item as complete by clicking its checkbox. You can optionally link it to the corresponding timeline entry — this creates a permanent connection between the planned procedure and the clinical record of its execution.

Plan Statuses

StatusMeaning
DraftPlan created, not yet presented to patient
ActivePlan accepted; treatment in progress
CompletedAll items marked complete
CancelledPlan abandoned; retained for history
Cost tracking The plan automatically totals item costs and shows how much has been "completed" vs. still outstanding. This is for reference only — DentVault does not issue invoices.

Chapter 6 Dental Chart

The dental chart is a fully interactive SVG diagram of all 32 teeth. Click any tooth to view and edit its condition, surface tags, notes, and history. The chart uses FDI notation throughout.

Reading the Chart

Each tooth is drawn with a crown (divided into 5 surfaces) and roots. The fill colour of each surface reflects the assigned clinical tag:

Condition / TagVisual appearance
HealthyWhite crown, no fill
CariesSurface filled with the caries colour (default: dark amber)
FillingSurface filled with the filling colour (default: blue)
CrownFull crown fill, outlined border
Root CanalLight purple crown + purple obturated canal line in roots
ImplantGray fixture rectangle with thread lines; no roots drawn
ExtractedDiagonal X across the tooth outline
MissingDashed tooth outline

Selecting and Editing a Tooth

  1. Click any tooth in the SVG chart. It highlights in blue and the detail panel opens on the right.
  2. In the detail panel, click the overall condition button that matches the tooth's status.
  3. To tag individual surfaces, click a surface cell in the 3×3 grid (Buccal, Occlusal, Lingual, Mesial, Distal). Multiple surfaces can be selected by dragging.
  4. With surfaces selected, click a tag button to apply that tag to all selected surfaces.
  5. Add freeform notes and a last examined date. These auto-save after a short pause.
DentVault 6 — Dental Chart

Charting Mode

Charting mode guides you tooth by tooth in FDI order (18 → 11 → 21 → 28 → 38 → 31 → 41 → 48), so you can chart the whole mouth without using a mouse.

  1. Click Charting starten in the chart toolbar. The progress bar appears.
  2. The first tooth (FDI 18) is highlighted in amber on the chart. Its detail panel opens automatically.
  3. Press a letter shortcut (e.g. k for Karies, f for Füllung) to apply a condition.
  4. Press Enter to advance to the next tooth, or Shift+Enter to go back.
  5. Click Fertig or press Escape to end charting at any time.

Bridges & Prosthetics

To record a bridge, drag across the abutment and pontic teeth on the SVG chart:

  1. Hold and drag from the first abutment to the last abutment tooth in the chart.
  2. The Restoration Editor panel opens. Toggle each tooth between Abutment and Pontic (middle teeth default to Pontic).
  3. For implant-supported bridges, click the abutment tooth and toggle Implant abutment.
  4. Click Confirm. The bridge bar appears on the chart connecting the selected teeth.

To dissolve a bridge, select any tooth in the group and click Brücke auflösen in the detail panel.

Chart Snapshots

Click the Snapshot button in the chart toolbar to record the current state of all teeth against a timeline entry. Snapshots are viewable from the timeline and allow you to see how the dentition changed over time.

Chapter 7 Periodontal Probing

The periodontal probing chart records a full 6-point examination for all 32 teeth. Each exam session is stored as a separate record, allowing you to track pocket depth changes over time.

Open the probing chart by clicking Perio in the patient timeline toolbar.

Reading the Probing Chart

The SVG chart displays all 32 teeth with vertical bars representing pocket depths. The upper arch (buccal above, lingual below) is separated from the lower arch by a horizontal line.

Bar colourPocket depthClinical significance
Green1–3 mmWithin normal limits
Amber4–5 mmIncreased probing depth; monitor
Red≥ 6 mmDeep pocket; active periodontitis likely

Each bar can carry additional markers:

  • Red dot at bar tip — Bleeding on probing (BOP) is positive
  • Yellow dot at bar base — Plaque present at that site
  • Purple segment below the bar — Recession depth (the bar + purple segment = CAL)

The Data Entry Panel

Click any tooth on the SVG chart to open its data entry panel on the right. The panel shows one tooth at a time with all six sites arranged in two groups:

RowInput typeDescription
PDNumber (0–15 mm)Pocket depth at each site
RecNumber (0–15 mm)Recession depth (leave blank if not measuring)
CALComputedClinical Attachment Level = PD + Recession (auto-calculated)
BOPToggle circleRed = bleeding on probing at that site
PlqToggle circleYellow = plaque visible at that site

Mobility & Furcation

Below the site measurements, the panel shows additional tooth-level findings:

FieldValuesNotes
Mobility0, I, II, IIIShown for all teeth
Furcation0, I, II, IIIShown only for multi-rooted teeth (molars, upper premolars)
Furcation sitesB / ML / DL (upper); B / L (lower)Appears when furcation grade > 0

Guided Charting Mode

For full-mouth recordings, use guided charting mode to move systematically through all 32 teeth without touching the mouse.

  1. Click ▶ Start Charting in the toolbar. The progress bar shows 0/32.
  2. The first tooth (FDI 18) is highlighted in amber on the chart and its panel opens.
  3. Enter PD values by tabbing through the six site inputs (MB → B → DB → ML → L → DL).
  4. After the last input (DL), press Enter to advance to the next tooth automatically.
  5. Use Shift+Enter to go back to the previous tooth.
  6. Press Escape or click ✓ Fertig to exit charting at any time.
Assistant-aided charting With guided charting mode, a dental assistant can call out values while the operator enters them — the flow matches the clinical sequence exactly (upper right → upper left → lower left → lower right).

Comparing Records Over Time

Use the Compare dropdown in the toolbar to overlay a previous exam's data as ghost bars on the SVG chart.

  1. Open the probing chart for the patient.
  2. Use Load to load the most recent exam record.
  3. Use Compare to select an earlier record as the baseline.
  4. The SVG chart now shows ghost gray bars (previous) behind the coloured bars (current).
  5. The comparison strip at the bottom shows: X sites improved, Y worsened, Z unchanged with a per-tooth breakdown.

Summary Statistics Bar

The bar at the very bottom of the probing chart dialog shows live statistics for the current record:

StatisticDescription
SitesNumber of sites recorded out of 192 total
Mean PDAverage pocket depth across all recorded sites
PD ≥ 4Count and percentage of sites with PD 4 mm or more
PD ≥ 6Count and percentage of sites with PD 6 mm or more
BOPPercentage of recorded sites with bleeding on probing

Chapter 8 Documents & Attachments

The Documents section stores any file type associated with a patient — X-rays, photographs, referral letters, consent forms, lab reports, and more. Files are stored inside the vault folder in a structured directory.

Adding Documents

  1. In the Documents section, click Add Document or drag files directly into the grid.
  2. Select a category for the document (e.g. X-ray, Referral, Photo).
  3. Optionally add a title and date.
  4. The file is copied into the vault and a thumbnail is generated for images.

Viewing Documents

Click any document thumbnail to open a full-screen viewer. Images are displayed inline; other file types are opened in your system's default application.

Document Categories

Categories are fully configurable in Settings → Document Categories. The filter pills at the top of the Documents grid update automatically when you add or rename categories.

Vault folder structure Documents are stored at vault/patients/[patient-id]/documents/[category]/filename. You can open the vault folder in Finder/Explorer to access files directly.
DentVault 8 — Documents & Attachments
Chapter 9 Dashboard & Analytics

The Dashboard provides a practice-wide overview of clinical outcomes. All statistics are computed from the structured data in your timeline entries — this is why accurate category and outcome tagging matters.

Summary Cards

The top row shows key practice metrics at a glance: total patients, entries this month, overall success rate, and active treatment plans.

Category Bar Chart

Shows the volume of entries per treatment category over the selected time period. Click any bar to drill down into the individual entries for that category.

Outcome Table

A matrix of treatment categories (rows) vs. outcomes (columns). Each cell shows a count. Click any cell to see the individual entries matching that category+outcome combination.

Filtering the Dashboard

Use the filter bar at the top of the Dashboard to restrict statistics to a date range or a specific doctor. This allows per-provider outcome analysis.

Example analyses
  • Filter by Endodontics + date range → see your root canal success rate over time
  • Filter by doctor name → compare outcomes between providers
  • Click Failed → Extracted in the Prosthodontics row → see which crowned teeth were eventually lost

Chapter 10 Reports & CSV Export

The Reports page gives you a filterable flat table of all timeline entries across all patients. Use it to generate practice activity reports, audit exports, or referral letters.

Available Filters

FilterDescription
Date rangeShow entries between two dates
CategoriesOne or more treatment categories
OutcomesOne or more outcome types
DoctorEntries attributed to a specific provider
Tooth numbersEntries involving specific FDI tooth numbers

Exporting to CSV

  1. Apply filters to narrow the entries as needed.
  2. Click Export CSV at the top right.
  3. A .csv file is saved to your Downloads folder. It can be opened in Excel, Numbers, or any spreadsheet application.

The CSV includes: patient name, date, category, outcome, tooth numbers, doctor, and the entry text.

Use case: insurance reporting Filter by date range + category (e.g. Endodontics), then export to CSV to prepare treatment summaries for insurance submissions.

Chapter 11 Settings

Settings is where you customise DentVault for your practice. Everything that has a "type", "category", or "label" can be renamed, extended, or removed here. Changes take effect immediately across the whole app.

Document Categories

The categories available when attaching a document (e.g. X-ray, Referral, Consent Form). Changes here automatically update the category filter pills in the Documents section and the vault folder structure.

Clinical Tags

Two separate tag lists:

  • Acute Problem Tags — used in the Acute Problems box on the patient profile (e.g. Pain, Swelling, Abscess)
  • Medical History Tags — used in the Medical History box (e.g. Diabetes, Hypertension, Anticoagulants)

Staff Roles

The role labels that appear next to staff names throughout the app (e.g. Principal Dentist, Associate, Hygienist, Oral Surgeon). Rename or add roles to match your practice structure.

Text Blocks

Reusable text snippets inserted with the / command palette in any entry text field. Add standard templates for post-operative instructions, recall reminders, referral language, and more.

Complication Types

The structured complication types that can be logged against a timeline entry (e.g. Post-operative Pain, Instrument Separation, Allergic Reaction). 13 defaults are provided. You can rename, add, or delete types.

Dental Tag Shortcuts

Accessible via Edit Tags in the dental chart toolbar. Each tag has a configurable single-letter keyboard shortcut for use during charting mode. Duplicate shortcuts are blocked on save.
